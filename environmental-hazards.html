Environmental Hazards

Mutants are not the only threats in this new world; recognize and avoid these environmental traps!

Upon arriving on Earth, the Voidborne virus quickly spread across the world, infecting every suitable host it could reach. Within weeks, billions of ravenous mutants emerged and began hunting any living beings they could overpower, including humans. Metropolises fell one by one, overrun by hordes of mutants that easily crushed conventional defenses. In response, hundreds of tactical nukes were deployed as as desperate attempt to stymie the mutant advance. Although somewhat effective, this strategy caused massive environmental devastation. Today, survivors must tread carefully, as the planet remains scarred by deadly environmental hazards.

Radiation Zones

Radiation zones are pockets of deadly fallout left by the tactical nuclear strikes. These areas emit intense ionizing radiation that saturate the soil, air, and even the ruins of buildings. Prolonged exposure can cause acute radiation sickness, marked by nausea, vomiting, bleeding, and eventual organ failure. Even brief contact without protection can damage DNA and weaken the immune system. Survivors attempting to cross these radiated deadlands must don specialized protective gear such as lead-lined suits, sealed respirators, and dosimeters (to monitor exposure levels).

Radioactive Dust Clouds

Radioactive dust clouds swirl across the scarred landscape, sometimes blanketing entire regions. These clouds are composed of fine radioactive particles that cling to clothing and infiltrate lungs with each breath. Survivors exposed to the dust may suffer from chronic coughing, respiratory collapse, cancers, or acute radiation sickness. The dust also contaminates the land, ravaging food and water sources. Entire communities have been displaced by these clouds.

Toxic Wastelands

Toxic wastelands have formed where chemical plants, factories, and urban centers were destroyed. The corrosive acids that have seeped into the soil and the poisonous compounds that permeate the air have rendered these areas lifeless and dangerous. Exposure can cause immediate burns, respiratory failure, and long-term poisoning. The toxins also impair mobility and decision-making, making these areas even harder to cross. To safely navigate toxic wastelands, survivors must wear full-body protective suits resistant to chemical burns, along with respirators or gas masks to filter the poisonous air.

Infected Water Sources

Numerous water sources across the globe have been contaminated with the toxic chemicals from the nuclear fallout. Rivers, lakes, and reservoirs that once sustained life are now breeding grounds for disease and poisoning. Drinking or even coming into contact with these waters risks infection or toxic exposure. Even minimal contact can lead to severe infections, acute poisoning, or long-term health complications such as organ failure and cancers. Because of these dangers, survivors are advised to purify the water from any unknown sources before consumption.

Unstable Terrain

The once-solid earth is now fractured and unreliable due to the force of nuclear blasts and subsequent seismic activity. Sinkholes, fissures, and crumbling buildings are common, which pose a constant threat to those trying to navigate the landscape. One wrong step can lead to a fatal fall or being trapped beneath collapsing debris. Exploring ruins without climbing gear like grappling hooks and rope is strongly discouraged.
