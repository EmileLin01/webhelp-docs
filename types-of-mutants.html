Types of Mutants

Mutants can be classified as medium, large, and human.

Though all mutants are products of the Voidborne virus, their forms and behaviors vary depending on the host species. Mutants fall into three broad categories:

Medium Mutants

Anything larger than a house cat but smaller than a large dog is considered a medium mutant. Built for agility, medium mutants specialize in hit-and-runs, feints, and acrobatic maneuvers. The most dangerous variants arise from social species, as their pack behavior enables coordinated assaults and rapid reproduction. Given enough time, large groups of these social mutants can eliminate other mutant species within a wide territory. When food grows scarce, they shift their focus to more formidable prey, which includes human settlements.

While a lone medium mutant is generally vulnerable to sustained small-arms fire and poses little threat to a seasoned group, coordinated packs have been known to raze poorly defended colonies and settlements. Pack-minded medium mutants rank among the top threats in the post-apocalyptic world. Developing strategies and technologies to eliminate them before their numbers become unmanageable is key to humanity’s fight for survival.

Large Mutants

The large mutant's brute strength and sheer ferocity more than compensate for their typically solitary nature. With forelimbs powerful enough to uproot small trees, jaws that can tear through metal fencing, and hides resilient enough to absorb bullets, these mutants are almost impossible to deter and will audaciously attack anything they think they can overpower.

Though large mutants are comparatively rare, any sighting should be treated with extreme caution, with immediate retreat being the advised course of action. Artillery was once considered the most reliable way of neutralizing them, but newer mutants have evolved the ability to launch steel-piercing bone spikes from their mouths or backs. In light of this development, human settlements are increasingly turning to traps and mines as their primary means of defending against large mutants.

Human Mutants

Human mutants may not be the strongest or the fastest, but they are certainly the most cunning—and every bit as bloodthirsty. Once infected, the host is stripped much of their ingenuity, yet retains enough cognitive ability to understand and use certain tools. For instance, they have been seen wearing scavenged bullet proof vests in combat, bolstering their already superhuman durability. They are even capable of picking up and firing guns.

It is worth emphasizing that while human mutants may still vaguely resemble humans in form, any trace of humanity has been completely wiped out. The recollections of their previous lives have been wholly supplanted by a feral urge for wanton destruction. Therefore, anyone confirmed to be infected with the virus must be terminated before mutation occurs, and fully mutated individuals should be eliminated on sight without hesitation or exception.
