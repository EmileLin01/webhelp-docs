Essential Survival Skills

A new world requires a new set of skills. Master them to survive in this unforgiving land.

As modern infrastructure has largely collapsed in the wake of the Voidborne infection, knowing how to survive with minimal reliance on a support system has become paramount. Below are a few survival skills you will need to secure food, water, shelter, and safety in a world where humans are no longer on top of the food chain.

Fire Starting

Being able to create fire in the wilds without relying on modern tools is critical for warmth, cooking, and signaling.

  1. Gather dry tinder (e.g., dried grass, bark shavings, etc.).
  2. Use a flint and steel or a bow drill to create sparks.
  3. Blow gently on the tinder until it ignites.
  4. Feed the flame with small sticks, then larger wood.

Note: Scout your surroundings before starting a fire in the wilds, as the flame can attract unwanted attention!

Finding Potable Water

Water sources that you come across in the wilds may be non-potable or contaminated, so knowing how to purify it is crucial.

  1. Locate a water source such as a river, stream, or rain catchment.
  2. Filter the water using cloth to remove sediment.
  3. Boil the water for at least one minute to kill pathogens.

Foraging

Not everyone may be cut out for hunting mutants, but there's no excuse for not knowing how to forage for berries and fruits.

  1. Scout a natural area with diverse plant life, such as edges of forests, riverbanks, or meadows.
  2. Check the fruit for pests, mold, or signs of rot before harvesting.
  3. Rinse the fruit with clean or boiled water before eating.
  4. Try a small amount first and wait several hours to rule out any adverse reaction.

Basic First Aid

Injury and infection are common threats. In the absence of modern medicine, knowing basic wound care will significantly increase your lifespan.

  1. Make sure the area is clear of hazards like fire, debris, or hostile threats.
  2. Control any bleeding by applying firm, direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage.
  3. Clean minor wounds with boiled (cooled) water or clean drinking water.
  4. Apply a bandage, cloth, or strip of fabric to cover the wound and protect it from infection.
  5. Monitor for signs of infection such as swelling, heat, pus, or red streaks.
